DEER LAKE RECREATION AREA

Deer Lake is a private recreational area exclusively for Blue Mountain property owners and their guests. This section outlines access requirements, rules, and amenities available at this beautiful community resource.

LOCATION & ACCESS

DEER LAKE LOCATION

Address: 3367 Blue Mountain Road, Linden, Virginia 22630

Deer Lake is nestled in a peaceful setting on Blue Mountain Road. The lake features a beach area, swimming area with a dock, picnic facilities, and fishing opportunities. It serves as a central gathering place for community events and a recreational retreat for residents.

EXCLUSIVE ACCESS

Deer Lake is for Blue Mountain property owners, their renters, and guests. Access is restricted to ensure the area remains a private amenity for our community members.

Access Requirements:

  • A Blue Mountain Recreational Area Pass is required when using common areas
  • Guests under 18 must be accompanied by the property owner
  • Renters must obtain passes from their property owner
  • Those without a pass may be asked to leave or reported for trespassing

OBTAINING YOUR PASSES

To receive your recreational area passes, email bmpoadeerlake@gmail.com with documentation verifying property ownership. Passes are typically mailed annually in May before the summer season begins.

ALTERNATIVE ACCESS OPTIONS

If you don't have your pass with you, there are backup options:

  • Present a valid driver's license or ID with an address matching property records
  • During summer months when security personnel are present, they can verify your status on the homeowner roster
  • In some cases, a property tax statement showing Blue Mountain ownership may be accepted

These alternative methods are provided as a convenience but are not guaranteed. The best practice is to always carry your official recreation pass.

HOURS OF OPERATION

Deer Lake recreation area is generally open from dawn to dusk. Swimming is permitted when conditions are safe and appropriate. During the summer season (Memorial Day through Labor Day), the beach area may have more restricted hours which will be posted at the entrance.

RECREATIONAL AREA PASSES

PASS DISTRIBUTION

Each property owner is issued a set number of recreational area passes, typically:

  • Two permanent passes for property owners
  • Additional guest passes as determined by the Board
  • Special passes may be issued for events with advance notice

Passes are usually mailed to property owners in good standing (current on Sanitary District taxes) each spring before the summer season begins. If you haven't received your passes by June 1, or if you're a new property owner, contact bmpoadeerlake@gmail.com.

GUEST POLICY

Residents should use good judgment when inviting guests to Deer Lake. The guest policy includes several important guidelines:

  • Property owners must be present with their guests at all times
  • Guests under 18 must be accompanied by an adult property owner
  • The number of guests should be reasonable to avoid overcrowding
  • Frequent large gatherings may inconvenience others and raise concerns about improper use
  • The Executive Board reserves the right to review and amend guest policies as needed

IMPORTANT NOTE

Selling access to Deer Lake is strictly prohibited. Property owners who allow their passes to be used by unauthorized individuals or who misuse guest privileges may have their recreation privileges suspended.

RENTAL PROPERTY CONSIDERATIONS

If you rent your Blue Mountain property to others, special considerations apply to Deer Lake access:

  • Renters may use the recreational area with passes provided by the property owner
  • Property owners are responsible for ensuring renters understand and follow all rules
  • Long-term renters may request dedicated passes with written permission from the property owner
  • Short-term vacation renters should be given temporary passes that are returned at the end of their stay
  • Property owners remain responsible for the conduct of their renters at all community facilities

SECURITY PERSONNEL

During peak summer months (Memorial Day through Labor Day), security personnel may be present at Deer Lake on weekends to check passes and enforce rules. They are there to ensure the safety and enjoyment of all property owners and their guests. Please be courteous and cooperative if they request to see your pass or remind you of any rules.

LAKE & BEACH RULES

To ensure everyone's safety and enjoyment, the following rules have been established for Deer Lake. These rules are also posted at the lake entrance and are enforced year-round.

GENERAL RULES

  • Recreational Area Passes must be visible when using the lake area
  • Swimming is at your own risk ‚Äì no lifeguard on duty
  • Children must be supervised at all times
  • Loud music or disruptive behavior on beach or dock is prohibited
  • No fishing from the beach at any time
  • All trash must be taken home ‚Äì no trash receptacles provided
  • No glass containers permitted in beach or swimming areas
  • No motorized boats allowed on the lake

DOG POLICY

To maintain a safe and enjoyable environment for all visitors:

  • No dogs allowed on weekends and holidays during Memorial Day through Labor Day
  • Weekdays (Monday-Friday): Dogs are allowed near the dam area but not on the dock
  • All dogs must be leashed at all times
  • Owners must clean up after their pets immediately
  • Aggressive dogs will not be tolerated and must be removed

SWIMMING & WATER SAFETY

  • No lifeguard on duty ‚Äì swim at your own risk
  • Life preservers available for emergencies only (not toys)
  • Children under 12 must be accompanied by an adult in the water
  • No diving from the dock or any other structures
  • Inflatable devices should be appropriate for the user's swimming ability
  • Swimming is prohibited during thunderstorms

WATER QUALITY

Deer Lake is a natural body of water. While the lake is generally suitable for swimming, water quality can vary based on weather conditions and seasonal factors. Swimmers should avoid swallowing lake water and shower after swimming.

FISHING REGULATIONS

  • Virginia fishing license required for all anglers 16 and older
  • Catch and release fishing is encouraged to maintain fish populations
  • No fishing from the beach area at any time
  • Fishing from the dock is permitted when swimmers are not present
  • Please be courteous and move to allow swimmers access to the dock
  • Dispose of fishing line properly ‚Äì never leave it at the lake

ALCOHOL & MINORS

  • No underage drinking in recreation areas
  • Persons age 16 and under must be accompanied by an adult
  • Adults are responsible for the behavior of minors in their care
  • Excessive alcohol consumption that leads to disruptive behavior is prohibited

VEHICLES & DAM ACCESS

  • No unlicensed vehicles (ATVs, dirt bikes) on BMPOA roads or common areas
  • No driving or parking on the dam (Virginia law)
  • Park only in designated areas
  • Do not block access roads or turnaround areas
  • Speed limit on access roads is 15 mph

VANDALISM & TAMPERING

BMPOA signs and property are assets of the association. Any tampering, vandalism, or theft will be reported to law enforcement. Violators may lose their lake privileges and face criminal charges.

Deer Lake is a shared community resource. Please be respectful of other users, follow all posted rules, and help keep the area clean and safe for everyone to enjoy. Your cooperation helps maintain this beautiful amenity for current and future residents.
