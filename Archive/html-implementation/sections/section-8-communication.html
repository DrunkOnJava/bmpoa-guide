COMMUNITY COMMUNICATION

Staying connected with your neighbors and informed about community matters is essential to mountain living. This section covers our various communication channels, social activities, and ways to get involved.

SOCIAL EVENTS & ACTIVITIES

The BMPOA Social Committee works throughout the year to create opportunities for neighbors to connect, celebrate, and build our mountain community. These events foster the friendly atmosphere that makes Blue Mountain such a special place to live.

ANNUAL COMMUNITY EVENTS

The Social Committee sponsors four major community events each year, one per season:

  • Spring Community Cleanup & Potluck
    • Usually held in April after wood chipping
    • Morning cleanup of common areas
    • Potluck lunch at the Lodge
    • Great opportunity to meet new neighbors
  • Summer Independence Day Picnic
    • July 4th celebration at the Lodge
    • BBQ, games, and family activities
    • Evening fireworks viewing from the deck
    • Bring a dish to share
  • Fall Harvest Festival
    • October celebration of autumn
    • Pumpkin carving and decorating
    • Cider and seasonal treats
    • Costume parade for kids
  • Winter Holiday Gathering
    • December holiday party
    • Cookie exchange
    • Carol singing and fellowship
    • Collection for local charities

ONGOING ACTIVITIES

YOGA AT THE LODGE

Join your neighbors for weekly yoga sessions suitable for all skill levels. Classes are held year-round with schedules posted on the BMPOA website and Facebook group. Bring your own mat and water bottle.

COMMUNITY CLEAN-UP DAYS

Throughout the year, volunteers gather to maintain and beautify our common areas. These work sessions are great opportunities to contribute to the community while getting to know your neighbors. Tools and refreshments provided.

FACEBOOK GROUPS & ONLINE RESOURCES

Blue Mountain residents stay connected through several online platforms. While the official BMPOA website remains the authoritative source for association business, these social media groups provide valuable day-to-day communication among neighbors.

BLUE MOUNTAIN POA FACEBOOK GROUP

PRIVATE COMMUNITY GROUP

This private Facebook group is exclusively for BMPOA members and provides a platform for:

  • Informal community updates and discussions
  • Event announcements and reminders
  • Neighbor-to-neighbor assistance
  • Lost and found postings
  • Recommendations for local services

Note: This group is not officially sponsored by BMPOA. For official information, always refer to www.bmpoa.org

BLUE MOUNTAIN INFO EXCHANGE

COMMUNITY INFORMATION FORUM

"The purpose of this forum is to foster a sense of community among our neighbors and exchange information that is pertinent to the mountain (Linden, VA). We are all neighbors, regardless of whether or not we've met each other in person. Blue Mountain is a wonderful place to be and let's hope that this group makes it a little easier for everyone to keep up to date on what's going on."

Common topics include:

  • Weather alerts and road conditions
  • Wildlife sightings and safety concerns
  • Power outages and utility issues
  • Contractor and service provider referrals

BLUE MOUNTAIN RESCUE BRIGADE

EMERGENCY SUPPORT NETWORK

"Blue Mountain Rescue Brigade...is a group of us who want to do our best to ensure the wealth, life, property and safety of our mountain friends. Be it the 2-legged kind or the 4-legged type. This is intended to HELP local emergency crews and law enforcement, NOT REPLACE IT. Everyone should still dial 911 first. Then 'light-up' this site."

Remember: Always call 911 first in emergencies, then post to the Rescue Brigade for additional neighbor support.

NEWSLETTER & ANNOUNCEMENTS

The BMPOA Newsletter serves as our primary written communication to all property owners. Published quarterly, it provides comprehensive updates on community matters, upcoming events, and important announcements.

NEWSLETTER CONTENT

Each issue typically includes:

  • President's Message: Updates from the Board President on current initiatives and community matters
  • Board Meeting Highlights: Summary of key decisions and discussions from recent meetings
  • Committee Reports: Updates from Roads, Social, ARC, and other active committees
  • Upcoming Events: Calendar of community activities and important dates
  • Seasonal Reminders: Weather preparations, maintenance tips, and safety information
  • Community Spotlight: Features on neighbors, local businesses, or community achievements
  • Financial Updates: Budget status and Sanitary District information as needed

DISTRIBUTION

DELIVERY METHODS

  • Email: Primary distribution method for property owners with email addresses on file
  • Postal Mail: Printed copies mailed to those without email or by request
  • Website: PDF versions archived on BMPOA.org
  • Facebook: Links posted to community groups

To update your contact preferences or ensure you're receiving the newsletter, email the Secretary at secretary@bmpoa.org

SUBMITTING CONTENT

The newsletter welcomes submissions from community members:

  • Event announcements
  • Photos of community interest
  • Safety tips or alerts
  • Local business recommendations
  • Historical stories about Blue Mountain
  • Wildlife or nature observations

SUBMISSION DEADLINES

Content is due by the 15th of March, June, September, and December for inclusion in the following month's issue. Submit items to newsletter@bmpoa.org with "Newsletter Submission" in the subject line.

URGENT ANNOUNCEMENTS

For time-sensitive communications between newsletter issues, the BMPOA uses:

  • Email blasts to the membership list
  • Posts on the BMPOA website homepage
  • Updates in community Facebook groups
  • Signs posted at key locations (Lodge, mailbox areas)
