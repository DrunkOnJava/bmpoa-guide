NEW HOME CONSTRUCTION

Building or renovating in Blue Mountain requires adherence to specific guidelines that preserve our community's character and natural beauty. This section outlines the requirements and approval process for all construction projects.

PRE-CONSTRUCTION REQUIREMENTS

These requirements work in conjunction with the Blue Mountain Declaration of Protective Covenants, Conditions & Restrictions and BMPOA Bylaws. All construction projects must receive approval from the Architectural Review Committee (ARC) before any work begins.

IMPORTANT: ARC APPROVAL REQUIRED

Before applying for any Warren County building permit or zoning variance, you must notify and receive approval from the ARC Chair. This includes all new construction, additions, exterior modifications, and accessory buildings.

SUBMISSION PROCESS

All submissions and questions go to the ARC Chair via the "Contact Us" form on BMPOA.org under "Homeowners." Include:

  • Your name and email address
  • Property address
  • Contractor information (must include sponsoring owner)
  • Detailed plans and specifications
  • Materials list with colors and samples
  • Site plan showing setbacks and tree removal

Response Timeline:

  • ARC Chair will respond within 30 days with:
    • Approval
    • Disapproval (with reasons)
    • Request for additional information
  • If additional information requested, owner has 30 days to supply
  • ARC has 30 days to respond after receiving additional information
  • If no response within 30 days, request is deemed approved

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

SUBMISSION CHECKLIST

Building site plan with all setbacks clearly marked
Detailed architectural plans and elevations
Complete materials list with manufacturer and color specifications
Tree removal plan identifying all trees to be removed
Grading and drainage plan
Driveway location and construction details
Well and septic system locations (if applicable)

DEVIATIONS FROM APPROVED PLANS

Any deviation from approved plans risks legal action. If changes become necessary during construction, you must submit revised plans to the ARC for approval before proceeding with the changes.

BUILDING REQUIREMENTS

PERMITTED STRUCTURES

Each lot in Blue Mountain may contain:

  • One detached single-family dwelling
  • One private garage (attached or detached)
  • One accessory building (subject to requirements)

EXTERIOR MATERIALS & COLORS

APPROVED MATERIALS

  • Wood or wood-look siding (cedar lap, HardiePlank)
  • Solid-surface synthetics
  • Solid log construction
  • Stone or brick accents
  • Metal roofing (muted colors)
  • Architectural shingle roofing

PROHIBITED MATERIALS

  • Vinyl siding
  • T-111 or similar sheet siding
  • Concrete block (unless faced)
  • Bright or vibrant colors
  • Reflective metal finishes

Color Requirements: All colors must be muted and nature-reflective (greens, browns, tans, grays). No bright or vibrant colors are permitted. The ARC may request color samples before approval.

SETBACK REQUIREMENTS

MINIMUM SETBACKS

  • From Route 638 centerline: 75 feet
  • From BMPOA road centerline: 70 feet
  • From side property lines: 20 feet each side
  • From rear property line: 25 feet

Note: The ARC/Board may grant exceptions to side/rear setbacks on a case-by-case basis

SIZE REQUIREMENTS

  • Minimum dwelling size: 1,000 square feet of living space
  • Manufactured homes: Must meet Class A design criteria
  • Garages and accessory buildings: Must match primary structure's design and materials

SITE CONSIDERATIONS

Construction must be sited unobtrusively:

  • Use natural flora to screen from access roads
  • Minimize tree removal to what's necessary for:
    • Foundation footprint
    • Septic field
    • Driveway access
  • Additional clearing requires specific ARC approval

Tree and vegetation management:

  • Trees felled during construction must be:
    • Staged unobtrusively, or
    • Removed from the property
  • Limited scenic view clearing may be allowed
  • Extensive clearing is not permitted
  • No permanent outside outhouses allowed

FIRE-RESISTANT MATERIALS

Fire-resistant materials are strongly advised for all exterior components including roofing, siding, and decking. This helps protect your investment and our community from wildfire risks.

ACCESSORY BUILDINGS & SPECIAL REQUIREMENTS

ACCESSORY BUILDING REQUIREMENTS

One accessory building per 1-acre lot (other than a garage) is permitted if it:

  • Complies with Warren County setback, size, and height ordinances
  • Matches the primary structure in style and materials
  • Is pre-approved in writing by the ARC

Warren County Definitions & Requirements:

  • Building: Any roofed structure supported by walls/columns
  • Permit Exception: Structures under 200 sq ft in side/rear yard don't require County permit but still need ARC approval
  • Height Limit: 12 ft average height (average of eaves to ridge)
  • Cannot be placed: Forward of primary residence's front plane
  • Setback from roads: 50 ft minimum from front property line or any road
  • Side setbacks: 10 ft minimum from side lot lines

NONCONFORMING STRUCTURES

Pre-1992 accessory buildings are grandfathered by the County but still require ARC pre-approval to avoid covenant violations.

FOUNDATIONS & ADDITIONS

  • Accessory buildings on pilings, skids, or slabs must be in side/rear yard
  • Structures within 4 ft of primary residence are considered additions and must:
    • Require County building permit
    • Meet primary structure setbacks and height requirements

FENCE REQUIREMENTS

Standard requirement: Only split-rail fences are allowed

Approval process:

  • Submit design and materials plan to ARC
  • Receive written approval before installation
  • Exceptions considered case-by-case based on compatibility

BUILDING ON UNPAVED ROADS

ROAD CONSTRUCTION REQUIREMENTS

If your lot is accessed by an unpaved road, the lot developer must construct the roadway within the platted easement to BMPOA specifications:

  • 3" compacted #3 gravel base
  • 2" crusher run surface
  • 10 ft minimum width between ditches
  • Proper grading and crowning
  • Culverts installed at low points

Once inspected and accepted by the BMPOA Roads Committee, maintenance will be assumed by BMPOA.

CONSTRUCTION TIMELINE

  • All exterior work must be completed within one year of construction start
  • This includes:
    • Siding installation
    • Exterior painting/staining
    • Roofing completion
    • Deck/porch construction

COMPLIANCE & ENFORCEMENT

FAILURE TO COMPLY

The BMPOA takes covenant enforcement seriously to maintain property values and community standards. The following process applies to violations:

ENFORCEMENT PROCESS

BMPOA/ARC notifies owner in writing of violation and required corrective action
Owner has 60 days to correct OR 20 days to submit alternative plan/timetable
ARC has 30 days to approve/disapprove alternative plan
No response from ARC within 30 days = deemed approved
If alternative denied, owner has 60 days to comply
Appeals may be submitted to Arbitration Committee within 30 days
If appeal denied, owner must comply within 30 days or face legal action

LEGAL ACTION

If compliance is not achieved through the above process, the Board may pursue legal action including:

  • Injunctive relief to stop or correct violations
  • Recovery of attorney fees and court costs
  • Daily fines for continuing violations
  • Liens against the property

COMMON VIOLATIONS TO AVOID

  • Starting construction without ARC approval
  • Deviating from approved plans
  • Using prohibited materials
  • Violating setback requirements
  • Excessive tree clearing
  • Bright or non-approved colors
  • Incomplete exterior work
  • Unapproved accessory structures
  • Improper construction staging
  • Non-compliant fencing

WORKING WITH THE ARC

TIPS FOR SMOOTH APPROVAL

  • Submit complete plans with your initial application
  • Include photos of similar homes you're using as inspiration
  • Provide material samples when requested
  • Attend an ARC meeting to discuss your project
  • Communicate any changes immediately
  • Ask questions before making assumptions
